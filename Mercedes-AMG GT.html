Mercedes-AMG GT

Anis Zafar      20/101/2024 19:30      130

The Mercedes-AMG GT is a high-performance sports car that epitomizes the essence of AMG's racing heritage. It features a 4.0L V8 biturbo engine, delivering up to 577 horsepower in the GT R model. Known for its stunning design, dynamic handling, and luxurious interior, the AMG GT is a formidable contender in the world of sports cars.

Price: $118,600

History and Evolution

2014-2019 (First Generation)

The AMG GT was launched as a successor to the SLS AMG, featuring a front mid-engine, rear-wheel-drive layout. It was praised for its aggressive design, powerful V8 engine, and track-focused performance. The model range included the GT, GT S, GT C, and the high-performance GT R.

2020-Present (Second Generation)

The current generation of the AMG GT continues to build on its predecessor's success with refined aerodynamics, enhanced performance, and advanced technology. The lineup now includes the AMG GT Black Series, which boasts a 720 hp version of the V8 biturbo engine and is the most powerful AMG GT to date.

Specifications:

Specification Details
Engine 4.0L V8 biturbo
Horsepower 469 hp (GT), 577 hp (GT R), 720 hp (GT Black Series)
Transmission 7-speed dual-clutch automatic
Fuel Economy 16 mpg city / 22 mpg highway
Seating Capacity 2
Infotainment 10.25-inch touchscreen, Apple CarPlay, Android Auto
Safety Features Active Brake Assist, Blind Spot Assist, Lane Keeping Assist, Adaptive Highbeam Assist
Additional Highlights The AMG GT also offers a range of performance features, including an AMG-specific suspension with adaptive damping, an electronic limited-slip differential, and a lightweight aluminum spaceframe. Optional features include carbon-ceramic brakes, a Burmester surround sound system, and the AMG Track Pace app for performance data logging.
